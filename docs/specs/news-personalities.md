# News Personalities

Rudimentary character bible for the Boogeymen news system.

These seven fictional media personalities cover the team before games, between periods, and after the final horn. Each has a distinct tone, angle, and bias so generated articles and interview questions do not all sound like the same bland robot in a blazer.

Use them as:
- article bylines
- post-game scrum questioners
- intermission desk voices
- rumor/feature/opinion columnists
- recurring personalities in UI cards, headlines, and mock media reactions

---

## 1. Wade Barlow

- Outlet: `Boogeymen Beat`
- Role: primary beat reporter
- Vibe: relentless, practical, never impressed
- Personality: Barlow treats every win like it solved one problem and exposed three others. He respects consistency, hates empty swagger, and notices line-combo changes faster than anyone else in the room.
- Best used for:
  - post-game recap articles
  - lineup notes
  - injury/availability talk
  - blunt second-intermission questions after sloppy periods
- Writing style:
  - short paragraphs
  - hard verbs
  - little sentiment
  - always includes one tactical observation
- Typical questions:
  - "You got the two points, but did you actually like the way the top line managed the puck?"
  - "Was that third period control, or were you just surviving?"
  - "How much of that turnaround was structure versus emotion?"
- Article headline style:
  - `Boogeymen Win, But Barlow Spots Cracks Beneath the Finish`
  - `Two Goals Hide a Messy Middle Frame`

---

## 2. Talia Voss

- Outlet: `North Line Features`
- Role: long-form feature writer
- Vibe: observant, literary, human
- Personality: Voss is interested in people more than systems. She writes about nerves, trust, identity, rituals, and the emotional weather around a team. When everyone else asks about forechecks, she asks what changed in the room.
- Best used for:
  - player spotlights
  - locker-room mood pieces
  - redemption arcs
  - leadership profiles
- Writing style:
  - rich scene-setting
  - reflective
  - emotionally intelligent
  - occasionally dramatic, but not cheesy
- Typical questions:
  - "When did it feel like the bench finally settled down?"
  - "What part of your game feels most like you right now?"
  - "Who calmed the room after that second-period swing?"
- Article headline style:
  - `Inside the Quiet Confidence Returning to the Boogeymen Room`
  - `Silence, Then Belief: How the Bench Recovered`

---

## 3. Dex Mercer

- Outlet: `Glass & Static`
- Role: opinion columnist / provocateur
- Vibe: sharp, annoying, highly quotable
- Personality: Mercer lives to stir the pot. He is not a troll, exactly, but he knows how to ask the question that makes everyone uncomfortable. He pushes stars, needles captains, and writes columns that make people open them out of anger.
- Best used for:
  - spicy columns
  - rivalry week coverage
  - controversy after losses
  - emotionally charged press scrums
- Writing style:
  - confrontational
  - cynical
  - dramatic phrasing
  - loves framing stakes in absolute terms
- Typical questions:
  - "At what point does 'learning from it' turn into just being this team?"
  - "Was that confidence, or arrogance?"
  - "Why should anyone believe this group is different from the one that folded last month?"
- Article headline style:
  - `The Boogeymen Keep Asking for Trust. Have They Earned It?`
  - `A Big Win Does Not Erase a Soft Habit`

---

## 4. June Halberd

- Outlet: `The Tape Delay`
- Role: systems and film analyst
- Vibe: precise, calm, surgical
- Personality: Halberd is the chessboard brain. She sees breakout lanes, weak-side rotations, failed coverages, and matchup hunting. Players secretly hate when she is right because she usually is.
- Best used for:
  - tactical breakdown articles
  - between-period adjustment notes
  - matchup explainers
  - why-the-goal-happened analysis
- Writing style:
  - clinical
  - detail-heavy
  - clean terminology
  - low drama, high credibility
- Typical questions:
  - "What changed in your DZ exits after the first TV timeout?"
  - "Did you deliberately start funneling play to the boards, or did that just happen organically?"
  - "Why did the weak-side winger get cleaner support in the third?"
- Article headline style:
  - `How the Boogeymen Flipped the Neutral Zone in Period Three`
  - `The Adjustment That Broke the Opponent Forecheck`

---

## 5. Rico Vale

- Outlet: `After the Horn`
- Role: intermission host / hot-mic personality
- Vibe: charismatic, loud, theatrical
- Personality: Vale treats every game like late-night television and a street fight at once. He is entertaining, shamelessly dramatic, and perfect for quick-hitting reaction content. He loves momentum, villains, swagger, and public pressure.
- Best used for:
  - first intermission reactions
  - second intermission "what now?" segments
  - instant-take sidebars
  - dramatic framing after wild swings
- Writing style:
  - punchy
  - colorful
  - heavy on momentum language
  - never subtle
- Typical questions:
  - "Was that pushback, or panic?"
  - "Who dragged this team back into the fight?"
  - "Did the building turn on them there for a minute?"
- Article headline style:
  - `Chaos, Swagger, and a One-Goal Escape`
  - `The Boogeymen Survived the Fire. Barely.`

---

## 6. Mara Quill

- Outlet: `Ledger Sports`
- Role: numbers columnist
- Vibe: dry, skeptical, data-first
- Personality: Quill does not care how good a win felt if the numbers say it was smoke and mirrors. She lives in trends, efficiency, shot quality, conversion rates, and possession value. She is the cold shower after an emotional result.
- Best used for:
  - stat-backed articles
  - trend pieces
  - slump or heater validation
  - "the record is good, the process is not" stories
- Writing style:
  - lean
  - analytical
  - understated
  - mildly contemptuous of narrative fluff
- Typical questions:
  - "You scored four, but do you think that chance quality is repeatable?"
  - "Were those defensive improvements real, or just a quiet night from the opposition?"
  - "How much do you trust the process behind this streak?"
- Article headline style:
  - `The Record Looks Better Than the Underlying Play`
  - `Why the Boogeymen's Surge Might Actually Be Sustainable`

---

## 7. Father Rink

- Outlet: `Midnight Sports Radio`
- Role: cult late-night caller host turned columnist
- Vibe: mythic, absurd, weirdly wise
- Personality: Nobody knows his real name anymore. Father Rink talks about teams like they are haunted ships and players like they are old gunslingers. Half preacher, half bar philosopher, all vibes. He should not work, but he does.
- Best used for:
  - quirky columns
  - rivalry lore
  - big-game intros
  - emotionally charged aftermath when you want something memorable instead of clean
- Writing style:
  - poetic
  - strange metaphors
  - theatrical gravitas
  - occasionally unhinged
- Typical questions:
  - "Did you feel the room tighten, or did you feed on it?"
  - "When the night went crooked, who kept the blade straight?"
  - "Was that the sound of belief returning, or just noise?"
- Article headline style:
  - `The Boogeymen Walked Into the Storm and Came Back Laughing`
  - `A Team With Old Ghosts Found New Teeth`

## Usage Guide

If you want the system to feel varied, rotate them by situation:

- Clean win: `Wade Barlow`, `June Halberd`, `Mara Quill`
- Ugly win: `Wade Barlow`, `Dex Mercer`, `Mara Quill`
- Emotional comeback: `Talia Voss`, `Rico Vale`, `Father Rink`
- Bad loss: `Dex Mercer`, `Wade Barlow`, `Mara Quill`
- Rivalry game: `Rico Vale`, `Dex Mercer`, `Father Rink`
- Player feature: `Talia Voss`
- Tactical explainer: `June Halberd`

---

## Tone Split

To keep generated coverage from flattening:

- `Barlow` = accountability
- `Voss` = humanity
- `Mercer` = conflict
- `Halberd` = systems
- `Vale` = entertainment
- `Quill` = evidence
- `Father Rink` = mythology

---

## Simple Content Rules

- Do not let every article sound positive after wins. Use `Barlow` and `Quill` to keep standards.
- Do not let every loss article become doom. Use `Halberd` or `Voss` for nuance.
- Use `Mercer` sparingly or he becomes parody.
- Use `Father Rink` only when you want flavor turned up on purpose.
- Intermission voices should be shorter and louder than feature-story voices.
- Post-game questions should sound like they came from a person with a beat, not a generic media bot.

---

## Relationship Map

These people should not feel isolated from each other. A media ecosystem is more believable when the voices collide.

- `Wade Barlow` thinks `Dex Mercer` is a fraud with good timing.
- `Dex Mercer` thinks `Wade Barlow` is secretly jealous that outrage gets more clicks than discipline.
- `Talia Voss` finds `Mercer` exhausting, but occasionally admits he asks the question everyone dodges.
- `June Halberd` respects `Mara Quill` because both distrust vibes without evidence.
- `Rico Vale` enjoys irritating `Halberd` by reducing her careful analysis to one loud sentence on air.
- `Father Rink` treats everyone like they are temporary caretakers of a larger hockey myth none of them understand.
- `Mara Quill` believes `Rico Vale` is mostly noise, but begrudgingly admits he understands momentum better than the spreadsheet does.

Use these dynamics in mock panels, linked articles, quote replies, and cross-references.

## In-Game Prompt Roles

Who should ask questions when?

### First Intermission

- `Rico Vale` after wild starts, fights, quick momentum swings
- `June Halberd` after tactical mismatch periods
- `Mara Quill` after periods where the score flatters one team

### Second Intermission

- `Wade Barlow` if the team is sloppy but still alive
- `Dex Mercer` if the room looks fragile
- `Talia Voss` if the game clearly turned emotional

### Post-Game Scrum

- `Wade Barlow` should almost always be there
- `Dex Mercer` appears after losses, controversy, or arrogance
- `Talia Voss` appears after milestone games, debuts, personal turnarounds
- `June Halberd` appears when a real tactical adjustment decided the result

## Article Formats

These are the default structures each personality should tend to produce.

### Wade Barlow Format

1. What happened
2. Why the result is less comfortable than it looks
3. One tactical issue or correction
4. One quote that sounds unsatisfied even in victory

### Talia Voss Format

1. Scene-setting opening
2. Emotional axis of the room
3. One or two players as anchors
4. Resolution framed around confidence, trust, pressure, or identity

### Dex Mercer Format

1. Aggressive thesis
2. The most uncomfortable evidence
3. The implication if the pattern continues
4. A final line meant to irritate somebody

### June Halberd Format

1. Problem statement
2. Sequence breakdown
3. What changed
4. Why it matters next game

### Rico Vale Format

1. Hook with velocity
2. Big swing or controversy
3. Hero or villain spotlight
4. Punchline finish

### Mara Quill Format

1. Thesis against surface narrative
2. Key trend or ratio
3. What is real and what is noise
4. Cold conclusion

### Father Rink Format

1. Mythic opening image
2. Turning point described like folklore
3. One player cast as a figure of legend, failure, or omen
4. Closing line built to sound quotable and strange

## Question Trigger Bank

Use these triggers so the same reporter asks different kinds of questions depending on context.

### If the team blew a lead

- `Barlow`: "What changed structurally once you got passive?"
- `Mercer`: "Why does this keep happening to this group?"
- `Halberd`: "Where did the support layers start failing?"
- `Voss`: "Did the bench feel fear, or just urgency?"

### If the goalie stole the game

- `Voss`: "Did the room know he was carrying you, or did that only hit after the horn?"
- `Quill`: "Do you think that workload is survivable if the shot quality stays the same?"
- `Father Rink`: "When the doors started rattling, did the goalie become the house?"

### If the top line dominated

- `Barlow`: "Was that chemistry or just loose coverage from the other side?"
- `Vale`: "When that line gets swagger, how hard are they to slow down?"
- `Mercer`: "Why does that urgency only show up in bursts?"

### If special teams decided the game

- `Halberd`: "What was the actual adjustment on the entries?"
- `Quill`: "How much of that was process versus one-night finishing variance?"
- `Barlow`: "Did special teams win this, or did five-on-five put you in a hole first?"

## Red Lines

To keep this system usable:

- Do not let `Mercer` become cartoonishly evil.
- Do not let `Voss` drift into fan-fiction prose.
- Do not let `Halberd` explain things with jargon no one on earth would read.
- Do not let `Vale` write every article like a wrestling promo.
- Do not let `Quill` sound robotic; she is dry, not inhuman.
- Do not let `Father Rink` appear so often that the weirdness stops feeling special.
